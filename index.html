about

Hi there! My name is Grace Muro, I was born and raised in Southern California. My passions include distance running, traveling and reading. I am currently working on developing my graphic skills while working full time at Starbucks as a Shift Supervisor.

I have experience interning at UC San Diego as a Computer Science Education Developer in the summer of 2017. In the duration of this internship I worked with a team of individuals developing cirriculum for AP Computer Science Principles.

In addition to this I spent five years working at Pizza Hut as a Shift leader and have spent four years currrently working at Starbucks as a Shift Supervisor. My specific store is a brand new store that opened a year ago. Since opening we have become the store with the fastest times out of 64 store in San Diego County.

The other experience I have is in Graphic Design and Web Design. In my teen years I designed and sold over 1000+ merchandise items on Redbubble of my own unique designs. This included managing my own store and optimizing the visibility through my own marketing. I have also designed Instagram highlight covers, made logos for a non-profit organization and sold my own digital and physical artwork

Even though my passion lies in Web Development and UX, I still enjoy Graphic Design and Social Media work as well

Single Site

This is one of my first projects. It is a single site showcase featuring a guesing game and a working contact form. I chose to base my site off of my favorite musician group at the time, 'NCT Dream'. My main gripe with sites that already existed about them is that they were all lacking the color and fun that the group brings to the table. I wanted to create something that represented their youthful and vibrant energy from the ground up.

First look at the site. Includes a functional navigation bar.Includes light and dark mode.Hovering over the images on the 'Members' sections brings up descriptions of each person.

Responsive album picker. Hover over an album and it gets larger. Clicking on it shows the album tracklist and pulls up the cover. Coded in Javascript. API Integration

This project was a challenge. Following my first site I wanted to create another site for a trilogy with one more after this. I based this one of of the group 'Aepsa'. The scope included integrating an outside API or JSON file. Additionally I had to have a carousel of images in the site. The main issue was deciding what API to use. I used my previous site as a base and coded the framework in HTML then went in and stylized it. Then I integrated the JSON file and the API.

The first look at the site. Navigation bar and introduction. Member introductions. Not part of the requirements but I added it for context. The JSON File integration. Loaded from an outside file. The file had all the information of the Album details. None of it was in the HTML. API integration for the carousel.

Full Stack Site

The last site of my musician trilogy. I put all my skills learned from the past two projects into this one. There were no limitations or scopes, only that it had to solve a problem.

The problem I chose to tackle with this site is that the music group I made my site on was an older group that has so much content from all over the internet since they have years of activity. I aimed to compile all that information in a single place so that newer fans can get information and content in a centralized place.

I followed my same process from the previous project, HTML first, then CSS, then some Javascript. I liked certain aspects from both sites but wanted to make this one even better. One of the main qualms I had with the first site is that while it showed the albums it didn't play music. I integrated the Spotify API to play songs when you click on an album.

The home page which has a functional navigation bar. A responsive member section that when hovered over includes description and overlay images A responsive Spotify API integration that plays the album title track when you click on it. When not signed into Spotify it plays a 13 second preview.
